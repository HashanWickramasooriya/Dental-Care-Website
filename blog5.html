How to Stop Cavities

By Dr. Sisira Jayakody in Chief Doctor, All Care Dental

September 13th, 2024

Understanding Cavities: What Are They?

Cavities are dental caries, or simply the holes in the teeth that result from bacteria...

Cavities: How Do They Develop?

The formation of cavities comprises three phases, which are:

  • Plaque Formation: Plaque is another layer that forms on the surfaces of your teeth...
  • Acid Production: Whenever you take foods rich in sugar...
  • Enamel Damage: The acid dissolves and damages your tooth enamel...

Ways to Stop Cavities from Forming

It is often said that an ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure, and this cannot be truer than in the case of cavity diseases. The following are many simple yet effective ways to stop cavities from developing:

Brush Your Teeth Regularly

Nevertheless, one of the simplest ways of avoiding cavities is brushing your teeth. Brush your teeth for a recommended 2 times a day, after waking up in the morning and before going to bed.

  • Use Fluoride Toothpaste: Fluoride helps build up the enamel and ward off bacteria.
  • Brush for Two Minutes: It is recommended that you should spend not less than two minutes brushing your teeth to cover every part of the mouth.
  • Replace Your Toothbrush Regularly: Replace your toothbrush every three months or when the bristles are non-functional.

Floss Daily

This is a very common technique that is usually considered a minor procedure, but without flossing, one can develop cavities. It also assists in cleaning those regions in between your teeth that a brush cannot reach.

  • Reach Tight Spaces: Flossing makes it easier to eliminate plaque and food particles that may have been left in between the teeth.
  • Prevent Gum Disease: Daily flossing will help you avoid gum diseases that may also lead to tooth decay.

Healthy Eating Habits to Stop Cavities

Every meal you take plays a crucial role, especially in the health of your teeth. Some foods cause tooth decay, and others promote oral health. Here they are:

Limit Sugary & Acidic Foods

Consumption of foods that are high in sugar and acid is a leading cause of tooth decay. These create conditions in which bacteria generate acid, which negatively impacts the enamel of the teeth.

  • Watch Out for Sugary Drinks: Sweetened beverages such as soda, sports drinks, and fruit juices contain a lot of sugar.
  • Avoid Sugary Snacks: Sweets like candies, cakes, and cookies are rich in sugar and can lead to tooth decay.
  • Limit Acidic Foods: Oranges, lemons, and citrus fruits can damage enamel if consumed frequently.

Eat Calcium-Rich Foods

Calcium strengthens teeth and prevents cavities. Consuming calcium-rich foods like dairy products, spinach, and kale increases the density of your teeth.

Dental Hygiene Practices for Cavity Prevention

Use Mouthwash

Mouthwash helps to remove leftover food and bacteria from areas a toothbrush and floss can't reach. Use fluoride mouthwash to protect teeth from cavities.

Chew Sugar-Free Gum

Chewing sugar-free gum increases saliva flow, rinsing off food particles and buffering acid in your mouth. Choose xylitol gum to reduce bacterial content in the mouth.

Regular Dental Checkups

Seeing a dentist regularly for cleanings and early detection of small cavities will help prevent major dental problems.

Treating Small Cavities Early

Fluoride Treatments

Fluoride can remineralize the enamel of small cavities and stop further decay. Dentists apply fluoride varnish or gel to affected teeth.

Dental Sealants

Dental sealants are coatings applied to back teeth to prevent cavities from forming. Sealants provide long-lasting protection for adults and children.

Final Thoughts

Prevention of cavities is better than treating them after they form. With regular hygienic practice, a proper diet, and routine checkups with the dentist, you can prevent cavities and maintain healthy teeth.
